# Case Study: Identity Theft

## What is Identity Theft?
Identity theft occurs when someone steals another person's personal 
information — such as their name, Aadhaar number, bank details, or 
college credentials — and uses it without permission, typically for 
financial gain or fraud.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. The attacker collects personal information through fake websites, 
   data breaches, or social media profiles where victims have 
   overshared details.
2. They use this information to access the victim's bank account, 
   apply for loans, or create fake profiles in the victim's name.
3. In some cases, attackers call victims pretending to be bank 
   officials or government representatives, convincing them to share 
   OTPs or Aadhaar details.
4. Once access is gained, money is transferred or credit is taken 
   out before the victim even realizes what has happened.

## Who is Usually Targeted?
College students and young adults are common targets because they 
are new to managing their own finances, frequently use public Wi-Fi, 
and often share personal details on social media without thinking 
about the risks.

## Consequences
The consequences of identity theft can be severe. Victims may lose 
money from their bank accounts, have their credit score damaged, or 
find loans taken out in their name. Recovering from identity theft 
is a long and stressful process that can affect academic performance 
and mental health.
